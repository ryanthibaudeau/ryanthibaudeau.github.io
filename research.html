// HRE-01

Hybrid Rocket Engines

Active Research

Hybrid rocket engines occupy a unique design space between solid and liquid propulsion, offering throttleability, restart capability, and improved safety over purely solid systems while avoiding some of the complexity of full liquid engines.

My research investigates the fundamental combustion and fluid dynamics that govern HRE performance — including fuel regression rate modeling, oxidizer injection effects, and combustion efficiency under varied operating conditions.

  • Fuel regression rate characterization
  • Oxidizer-fuel mixing and combustion efficiency
  • Combustion instability analysis
  • Scaling laws for hybrid motor design
// SFRJ-01

Solid Fuel Ramjets

Active Research

Solid fuel ramjets represent a compelling propulsion architecture for sustained supersonic flight — using ram air as the oxidizer source and a solid fuel grain as the energy source, eliminating the need to carry an onboard oxidizer.

My work explores the inlet-combustor coupling, fuel grain geometry effects, and flight envelope performance of SFRJ systems, with particular interest in combustion behavior across varying flight Mach numbers and altitudes.

  • Inlet aerodynamics and air capture efficiency
  • Solid fuel combustion in air-breathing flow
  • Fuel port geometry and regression uniformity
  • Performance modeling across flight envelopes
// space dynamics lab

Engineering Work

At Space Dynamics Lab, I contribute to propulsion engineering efforts that bridge the gap between research and operational systems. My role spans design support, analysis, and testing — applying the analytical rigor developed through PhD research to real mission needs.

This dual role provides a unique perspective: fundamental research informs engineering decisions, and engineering constraints sharpen research priorities. The interplay between the two environments is central to how I approach propulsion problems.
