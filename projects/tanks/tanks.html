// 01 — overview

Overview

Conventional propellant tanks are cylindrical or spherical — optimized for pressure vessels, not for the cuboid geometry of a small satellite bus. The result is wasted volume and a direct hit to the delta-V budget. There is also limited suppliers in set sizes, leading to high costs, long lead times, and difficulty in obtaining customization. This program developed a conformal, mission-specific Ti-6Al-4V propellant tank using laser powder bed fusion (LPBF) additive manufacturing, with a propellant management device (PMD) printed as an integral part of the structure rather than a separate assembly.

The design is sized to the satellite bus envelope from the start — filling the available propulsion bay and maximizing propellant volume. The same architecture scales from 1U CubeSat up to ESPA-class spacecraft. A half-ESPA configuration has been fully flight qualified and a patent application submitted, currently under review. The design in beginning stages of mass production with an industry partner under licensing agreements.

// 02 — qualification & results

Qualification & Results

The half-ESPA tank underwent a full qualification campaign — design and FEA verification, LPBF build on a Velo3D Sapphire system, post-print heat treatment and HIP, mechanical specimen characterization, CT inspection, precision cleaning, thermal cycling, helium leak checks, and proof pressure and burst testing. It passed all tests.

The design reduces lead time and cost by approximately 50% relative to conventional machined tanks. The integral PMD eliminates a separate assembly step, which is a large part of the reduced cost and lead time.

// Key Result

Half-ESPA configuration flight qualified, delivered to a commercial spacecraft customer, and licensed for mass production. Full-ESPA scaling demonstrated. Follow-on programs underway for additional form factors including a 1.5U CubeSat cold-gas integrated tank.
