Who I Am

I'm a propulsion engineer and PhD researcher at Utah State University's Propulsion Research Laboratory, where my work centers on advancing chemical propulsion systems — particularly hybrid rocket engines and solid fuel ramjets.

Alongside my academic work, I contribute to propulsion development at Space Dynamics Lab, applying research insights to real engineering problems and mission-critical systems.

My passion lies in understanding combustion dynamics, fuel regression, and the fluid-thermal interactions that define propulsion performance at the boundary of what's possible.

Core Research Areas

// HRE-01

Hybrid Rocket Engines

Investigating fuel regression behavior, combustion stability, and oxidizer-fuel interaction in hybrid propulsion systems for next-generation launch vehicles.

// SFRJ-01

Solid Fuel Ramjets

Exploring inlet-driven combustion, fuel grain geometry optimization, and flight envelope performance of solid fuel ramjet propulsion architectures.

// CHEM-01

Chemical Propulsion

Broad investigation of chemical propulsion fundamentals — thermodynamics, combustion chemistry, and system-level performance modeling and testing.
